--- title: The Cannon date: 2007-01-17T12:00:00-05:00 ---

There are days where I want to chuck it all and take up music.

I'd like to play the cannon.

I realize that would severly limit my repertoire to just two songs; "The 1812 Overture", and AC/DC's "For those about to rock", but I'm ok with that. Hyperspecialization is the new black.

I always assumed Simon and Garfunkel's "The Boxer" had cannon shots following its refrain. ("lie-la-lie *BOOM* lie-la-lie-lie, lie-la-lie")

It doesn't, but that's no reason for you not to branch out.

Posted by Mike Mariano on 2007-01-17

Over the summer I was browsing iTunes for a good recording of the 1812, as I'd moved at the start of the year and a lot of my (as-yet-unripped) CDs were in storage on the other end of the country. I found one whose artist listing was (I kid you not) "Churchbells of Gothenburg, Gothenburg Artillery Division, Gothenburg Symphony Brass Band, Gothenburg Symphony Chorus & Ove Gotting".

My first thought on seeing it was that more music needs to be scored for artillery division. It's an untapped market.
