Abstract

In the field of Project Management there are different chart types used to analyze a project, such as timelines, PERT, and Gantt charts. These different types of charts lack crisp mathematical definitions and thus it's not possible to categorize such charts or discuss their capabilities with any precision. This article provides a solid mathematical basis for describing such charts and then categorizes the most common types of charts based on their mathematical properties.
